# Indoor Footy Strategy Board — Glossary

## Sport

**Indoor Footy** — the indoor variant of Australian Rules Football (AFL). 7-a-side on a rectangular field, with goals at each end.

## Field

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Defensive end** | x=0, where the defending team's goal sits |
| **Attacking end** | x=100, where the attacking team's goal sits |
| **Arc / Crease** | A 20-unit-radius semicircle in front of each goal (the "goal crease") |
| **Pocket** | The area near the sideline inside the forward 50 (left/right pocket) |
| **Corridor** | The central band of the field, straight up the middle |
| **Wing** | The outer thirds of the field, near the sidelines |

## Game play

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Kick-in** | Restart from the defensive goal line after a behind or goal |
| **Press** | Defensive tactic where 3-4 players push up into the attacking half and play man-on-man, with 1-2 staying back to protect the goal |
| **Deep zone** | Defensive tactic where all players drop back inside the arc, compressing space around the goal. The specific team in these scenarios uses this aggressively — 6-7 players pack the arc |
| **Man-on-man** | Each defender marks a specific opponent rather than guarding space |
| **Sweeper** | A defender who holds the centre corridor behind the main zone, blocking the middle |
| **Fat side / open side** | The side of the field with fewer players — typically opposite the ball |
| **Lead** | A player runs from a deeper position toward the ball to receive a kick |
| **Finger break** | The area around the edge of the goal crease, near the pocket |
| **Set shot** | A kick for goal with time and space, no immediate pressure |

## Positions

| Label | Name | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Anchor | Deepest defender, helps set up play from the back (not a goalie) |
| **D** | Defender | Defensive position |
| **M** | Midfielder | Midfield, covers both ends |
| **F** | Forward | Attacking position around goal |
